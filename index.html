Why pass completion is a poor measure to evaluate a player’s passing ability.

Abstract

This piece of work presents an explanatory piece of analysis which focuses on changing perceptions on passing n football and how its reported. The analysis combines visualisations from tableau with… . I present a solution to how passing should be appraised in the future.

Introduction

In February 2020, Manchester City’s Rodri set the Premier League record for the most attempted and completed passes in a single match. Rodri completed 178 out an attempted 188 passes against West Ham United in their 2-0 win at the Etihad. While there is a lot of merit in being able to recycle possession of the ball. When pundit use pass completion or the amount of passes to compare player performance it really irritates me. This is because this metric does not incorporate the quality of the pass in question. Or the amount of passes made. In recent years Gundogan and Jorginho have broken the most passes record, and it often leads to headline such … biases towards sideways players? In this piece of analysis I’m going to integrate pass completion and why it’s a poor measure of players passing ability. Going to look at why every pass matters and how clubs should be looking in detail at every pass an opposition player makes not just an overall figure. While I’ll also provide an alternative ‘hypothetical’ solution to using pass completion.
